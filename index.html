Ewelina Węglarz-Tomczak

I am a biochemist, systems biologist and drug designer/medicinal chemist. I received PhD with Honours from Department of Bioorganic Chemistry at Wroclaw University of Science and Technology. I worked at Synthetic Systems Biology Group in Swammerdam Institute for Life Sciences at the University of Amsterdam before joining the Molecular Biology and Microbial Food Safety, where I am a parttime Senior Postdoc/Principal Investigator.

My research

My research is mainly focused on developing new methods for integrating gene expression/proteomics data with environmental factors such as nutrient availability (and/or microbiota) and studying cell metabolism. I use a genome-scale metabolic map as a modeling platform and cell lines as experimental models. Moreover, I work on developing computational methods for reverse engineering in biological systems, namely, parameter identification of dynamic models. Recently, in response to the pandemic of COVID-19, I lead a project on the development of the potential treatment using ebselen and its derivatives.
